# Playtesting Guide

---

## Playtest Types

| Type | When | Who | Duration |
|------|------|-----|----------|
| Self-test | Continuous | You | Ongoing |
| Friend test | Milestones | Friends/family | 30-60 min |
| Target audience | Pre-release | Actual players | Full session |

---

## What to Observe

### Quantitative
- Deaths per area/boss
- Time per section
- Resource usage
- Build/weapon choices
- Retry count

### Qualitative
- Facial expressions
- Verbal reactions
- Moments of hesitation
- Questions asked
- Features ignored

---

## Post-Playtest Questions

1. What was too hard?
2. What was too easy?
3. What felt unfair?
4. What was confusing?
5. What did you never use?
6. What was your favorite moment?
7. What would you change?

---

## Common Findings â†’ Solutions

| Finding | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| "Too hard" | Damage too high OR healing too rare | Reduce damage 10-20% first |
| "Too easy" | Player overleveled OR enemy HP too low | Add enemies before changing numbers |
| "Unfair" | Attack telegraphs unclear | Add/improve windup animations |
| "Boring combat" | Fights too long | Increase player damage |
| "One build dominates" | Balance gap | Buff weak options first |

---

## Iteration Rules

1. Change ONE variable at a time
2. Test with fresh playthrough
3. Document all changes
4. Save before/after versions
5. Trust data over assumptions
